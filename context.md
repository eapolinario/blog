# Context: Liminal Desiderata

## Project
**Liminal Desiderata** is a blog of technical essays and personal philosophy.

The writing lives at thresholds:
- between systems design and human judgment
- between engineering tradeoffs and values
- between what exists and what is desired but not yet realized

The audience is experienced engineers, technical leaders, and reflective practitioners.
Assume familiarity with software systems, tradeoffs, and abstraction.

Avoid beginner explanations unless explicitly requested.

---

## Core Concepts

### Liminal
From Latin *līmen* (“threshold”).

Used here to mean:
- transitional or in-between states
- periods where invariants are suspended
- moments before structure hardens into decisions

Examples:
- distributed systems during leader election
- organizations between phases of growth
- careers between identities
- designs before constraints fully collapse options

Liminal states are unstable, ambiguous, and generative.

---

### Desiderata
Plural of *desideratum* — “things desired but not yet attained.”

Used here to mean:
- articulated desired properties or outcomes
- goals that orient thinking rather than prescribe solutions
- ideals that may conflict or be only partially satisfiable

In engineering terms:
- desiderata are not requirements
- they may be mutually incompatible
- they guide tradeoff discussions rather than end them

---

### Liminal Desiderata (together)
The named things we want **while we are between stable states**.

This framing emphasizes:
- thinking before decisions calcify
- examining tradeoffs before they are hidden by narratives
- holding multiple incompatible goods in view at once

---

## Scope of Writing

Topics may include:
- distributed systems and infrastructure design
- abstraction, invariants, and failure modes
- career decisions and technical leadership
- tooling, productivity, and taste
- decision-making under uncertainty
- values encoded in systems

Writing should connect technical specifics to broader judgments.

---

## Tone and Style

- Precise, calm, and reflective
- Prefer clarity over cleverness
- No hype, no motivational fluff
- Avoid excessive jargon unless it adds precision
- Metaphors are welcome but must earn their keep

Write as if for peers you respect.

---

## Authorial Stance

- Curious but opinionated
- Skeptical of false certainty
- Comfortable with unresolved questions
- Willing to name tradeoffs explicitly
- Treats design and life decisions as structurally similar problems

---

## Instructions for the Assistant

When assisting with this project:
- Do not oversimplify
- Do not default to listicles or generic blog advice
- Challenge weak framing or vague claims
- Prefer concrete examples from systems or experience
- Optimize for long-term intellectual coherence, not virality

If a concept feels underdefined, ask to sharpen it.
If a claim hides a tradeoff, surface it.

The goal is durable thinking, not hot takes.
